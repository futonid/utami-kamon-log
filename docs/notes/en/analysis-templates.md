#### p2-grid-structure:

- A motif should be chosen and the work should suit that motif.

- **Source:** [Kamon](/docs/workbook/references/research.md#kamon)

### Result
#### Grid Calculation
- Divide the total area where the visual will sit using the golden ratio.
- Creating a central square of 1200/1.618 ≈ 741 pixels provides a frame that gives the design room to breathe.
- Margins (spacing) should be left from the edges of the page.
- Grid modules can be used.
- Small Units: A grid system divides a two-dimensional plane into smaller cells. These cells are called "areas" or "modules."
- Building Blocks: You place the parts of the plant motif you're designing (leaf, stem, etc.) inside these modules, or in areas formed by combining several modules.
- Calculation Example: After subtracting the margins (e.g., 100 pixels from each side) from your 1200-pixel area, you're left with a net area of 1000 pixels. If you want an 8-column grid, 1000 / 8 = 125-pixel square modules.
- **Source:** [Grid System](/docs/workbook/references/research.md#grid-system)
